## Vos manus viris monuistis.

---

### **Morphosyntactic Analysis:**

1. **Vos**  
   - **Part of Speech:** Personal Pronoun (2nd person plural)  
   - **Case:** Nominative  
   - **Gender:** Can be masculine or feminine (context-dependent)  
   - **Number:** Plural  
   - **Function:** Subject of the sentence ("You [all]")  

2. **manus**  
   - **Part of Speech:** Noun (4th declension, feminine)  
   - **Case:** Accusative  
   - **Gender:** Feminine  
   - **Number:** Plural  
   - **Function:** Direct object ("hands")  

3. **viris**  
   - **Part of Speech:** Noun (2nd declension, masculine)  
   - **Case:** Dative  
   - **Gender:** Masculine  
   - **Number:** Plural  
   - **Function:** Indirect object ("to/for the men")  

4. **monuistis**  
   - **Part of Speech:** Verb (Perfect tense, active voice)  
   - **Person & Number:** 2nd plural ("you [all] have advised/warned")  
   - **Mood:** Indicative  
   - **Tense:** Perfect (completed action in the past)  
   - **Infinitive:** monēre ("to warn/advise")  

### **Translation:**  
*"You (all) have warned the hands to/for the men."*  
*(This could be metaphorical, e.g., warning against violence, or literal, depending on context.)*  

---

### **IPA Pronunciation:**  
Classical Latin pronunciation (approximate):

- **viris** → [ˈwɪ.rɪs] (Note: "v" is pronounced as [w] in Classical Latin)  
- **monuistis** → [mɔ.nʊˈɪs.tɪs]  

---

### **Additional Notes:**  
- The verb **monuistis** is a contracted form of **monuivistis** (common in perfect tense forms).  
- The dative **viris** could imply "for the benefit of" or "to the detriment of" the men, depending on context.
